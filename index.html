EEG Descriptions

Neurologists should highlight the following

  • Dominant activity
  • Symmetric or asymmetric between hemispheres
  • Regular or irregular
  • Amplitude (voltages) of low, medium/moderate and high
  • Continuous/discontinuous
  • Predominant waveforms
  • Predominant voltages

An example can be: The background activity during waking state consists of well regulated medium (low to high) amplitude alpha activity at 10 Hz in the posterior head regions attenuated with eyes opening intermingled with low amplitude beta activity in the anterior head regions.

Results

Findings

The background activity during waking state consists of well regulated medium (low to high) amplitude alpha activity at 10 Hz in the posterior head regions attenuated with eyes opening intermingled with low amplitude beta activity in the anterior head regions.

Indications

This is a multichannel digital EEG recording using the international 10-20 placement system. The resting record is poorly organized but symmetric. A dominant posterior rhythm is seen but is poorly sustained. When present, it consists of a 10 hertz, 20 to 50 microvolt alpha rhythm. This attenuates with eye opening. During drowsiness, there is mild attenuation and slowing of the background rhythm. Stage II sleep was not achieved. Hyperventilation was not performed. Photic stimulation did not significantly alter the background rhythm. Prominent muscle artifact and movement artifact as well as electrode artifact was noted during this recording. There was noted also a single sharp left temporoparietal discharge.

Impression

Probably abnormal awake and sleep EEG for age due to brief runs of right frontal, sharply contoured delta activity. Sometimes, these frontal delta waves were maximal over the left frontal region, but more commonly over the right. Finally, occasional, irregular generalized spike-and-wave discharges or sharp and slow wave-like discharges were seen.
